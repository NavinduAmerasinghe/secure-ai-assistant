CWE-306: Missing Authentication for Critical Function

What it means:
This occurs when important functions (like changing passwords or deleting data) do not require users to prove their identity.

Why it is risky:
Attackers can perform sensitive actions without logging in, leading to data loss, account takeover, or system compromise.

Common insecure coding examples:
- Allowing password reset or admin actions without login.
- Exposing sensitive APIs without authentication.

Secure remediation guidance:
- Require authentication for all critical functions.
- Use strong authentication mechanisms.
- Regularly review access controls.

Relation to OWASP Top 10:
Missing authentication is covered under OWASP Top 10: A07:2021 – Identification and Authentication Failures.
