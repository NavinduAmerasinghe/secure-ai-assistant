CWE-327: Broken or Risky Cryptographic Algorithm

What it means:
This refers to using outdated, weak, or broken cryptographic algorithms (like MD5, SHA1, or DES) that attackers can break or exploit.

Why it is risky:
Weak cryptography can allow attackers to decrypt sensitive data, forge signatures, or bypass authentication.

Common insecure coding examples:
- Using MD5 or SHA1 for password hashing.
- Encrypting data with DES or RC4.

Secure remediation guidance:
- Use strong, modern algorithms (e.g., AES, SHA-256, bcrypt, Argon2).
- Keep cryptographic libraries up to date.
- Follow current security standards and recommendations.

Relation to OWASP Top 10:
Broken cryptography is covered under OWASP Top 10: A02:2021 – Cryptographic Failures.
